About me

Education

I attended STA (Saint Thomas Aquinas) high school and graduated on the first class honor roll. After high school I attended the University of Toronto while hoping to major in economics. Unfortunately I could not find much passion in what I was learning; so I returned home after my first year and decided to take a gap year until I found my passion. During my gap year; I was hired as a graphic design assistant. This fortunately resparked my interest in schooling; as I then applied to BCIT for their graphic design program.

Work Experience

During my gap year, I was worked for Tap and Barrel as a host and expo runner for a total of 8 months. I eventually left that job as I was offered an opportunity to work as a graphic design assistant for House of Knives. Since then; I have grown to take on more responsibility at my job; as I am now in charge of social media photography as well as creating new web designs for upcoming promotions.

Hobbies

I have a wide range of hobbies; but I would definitely put video games near the top of that list. I love anything from small indie studios works to anything published by AAA game studios. I also love anything to do with technology! Anything and everything associated with it; but mostly learning and staying up to date with the latest and greatest breakthroughs in tech.

Music

Music is one of; if not the most important thing to me in life. It has been such a helpful tool in all the highs and lows in my life; and I'm not quite sure what I would do without it. Some of my favourite artists/bands include: COIN, Twenty One Pilots, and The 1975.

Achievements

In grade 11 I was selected amongst 120 of my classmates to read a speech I had written to my entire school of 600 students. In that same year; I was the first student in STA history to be a 3 time BCTELA awards winner. In both grades 11 and 12; I made the first class honor roll (93% or above average) whilst being only one of three other students in my grade who were taking precalculus the same year as taking calculus.

Philosophy

Despite being raised a catholic my whole life; I am athiest to my core. I always struggled to believe in the concept of an omnipotent, all knowing "god." At least not in the way the bible described it. I did however like the idea that religions all have a moral way of thinking that guides how people live their lives. As a result; I became extremely interested in philosophy; and studied it extensively in toronto by taking 3 classes of it for my elective class choices. I seemeed to really latch onto the works of Immanuel Kant and his thoughts on what he described as the categorical imperative.
